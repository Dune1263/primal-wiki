Eternity

Overview

The Eternity Realm was the first realm ever created, born directly from the Eternal Consciousness. It is the frontier of absolute power, a plane where the True Gods dwell — beings who exist beyond time, matter, and form.

Purpose

From this realm, the gods observe, judge, and uphold the balance of all creation. Every decision taken here echoes across the other realms.

Access

Only beings of pure heart or spirit — or those who fulfil a divine purpose — may ascend. For mortals, Eternity is not just a realm… it is a state of existence.

Material Realm

Overview

The Material Realm was formed as a living masterpiece — a place where life could grow freely. It contains countless worlds, but Earth stands at its centre, the focal point where all elemental currents converge.

Purpose

Here, time flows, choices matter, and destinies intertwine. The Elementals shaped its lands and oceans, and later the mortals inherited their fragments of power.

Current Relevance

The Material Realm is the stage of the story — where Primal Energy, the heirs of Eternity, and the Heralds of Nether will decide the fate of all realms.

Void Realm

Overview

The Void Realm was designed as a neutral barrier between the Material Realm and the Nether. It is the place where the gods discard everything that does not deserve their attention: failed ideas, abandoned essences, fragments of reality not meant to exist.

Purpose

Its function is to separate chaos from life, preventing the influence of the Nether from contaminating creation. There is no light, no sound, no time — only endless stillness.

Relevance

Legends say that Vorlak’s essence wandered the Void for millennia, dormant, until it found a mortal vessel capable of unleashing his will.

Nether Realm

Overview

The Nether Realm was the first realm brought into existence after Eternity. It was forged as a prison for the Corrupted God — the one who tried to dominate creation and enslave the Elementals.

Purpose

Nether acts as a containment of chaos, where corrupted souls and discarded entities eventually end up. Its energy is dense, corrosive, and unstable.

Characteristics

Time flows erratically. Landscapes twist into storms of fire and ash. Inhabitants slowly lose their original form, becoming echoes of their own suffering.
